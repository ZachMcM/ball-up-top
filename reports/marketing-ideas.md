# Marketing Ideas — Ball Up Top

*Last updated: 2026-04-11*
*Post-pivot. Campus-community model. Location-based discovery. One court per campus.*
*Budget assumption: Near-zero cash. Primary resources are time, ambassador relationships, and product quality.*

---

## Strategic Frame

Ball Up Top's marketing is community seeding, not advertising. The goal at every campus is to make Ball Up Top feel like it already exists before most players have heard of it — so that when they do hear about it, they walk into a living community, not an empty app.

Every idea in this document serves that goal.

---

## Phase 1: Campus Launch Ideas

### Idea 1: The Founding Hooper Ambassador Program

**What it is:** Recruit 8–12 of the most respected pickup basketball players at the target campus rec center. Brief them before any public launch. They seed the court with 14 days of real sessions, ratings, and archetype data before launch day.

**Why it works:**
The cold-start problem is solved by manufactured critical mass, not organic growth. Ambassadors create the leaderboard that makes the app look alive on Day 1. More importantly: their names on the leaderboard create credibility. If the most respected player at the CoRec has an 86 overall, the rating system is validated for everyone who sees it.

**The ambassador pitch (revised):**
> "You're one of the best players at [Court]. I'm building the first leaderboard for campus pickup basketball. When we launch, your name is going to be at the top of that board. I want you to be a founding player — that status is permanent and disappears after launch. You in?"

**How to execute:**
1. Scout the court in person over 2–3 weeks. Identify the players others defer to.
2. Approach them directly — at the court, after a run, not via cold DM.
3. Show the app prototype. Demonstrate the archetype reveal moment specifically.
4. Get a 6-week commitment (2 pre-launch, 4 post-launch).

**Cost:** $0. Time: ~15 hours of in-person scouting and relationship building.

### Idea 2: "Who Runs Your Court?" Launch Campaign

**What it is:** Frame the launch around the one question every campus basketball community is already debating: "Who's the best player on campus?" Ball Up Top provides the answer. The launch campaign announces that the answer is now trackable.

**Campaign copy:**
- "The [School] leaderboard is live. Come see where you rank."
- "Who runs [School] basketball? Ball Up Top has the answer."
- "For the first time, [School] pickup basketball has a real ranking. Check in."

**Channels:** Instagram (launch post + story sequence), r/[school] (launch post), ambassador GroupMe drops.

**Why it works:**
The "best player at [School]" debate already exists. Ball Up Top doesn't invent the question — it gives it a home. Players who have opinions about campus basketball hierarchy will want to either validate or contest the leaderboard. Both motivations drive adoption.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 2–3 hours of content creation.

### Idea 3: The Court Takeover Day

**What it is:** On launch day or within the first week, 3–5 ambassadors show up to the court during peak hours (Saturday afternoon, weekday evening) with the explicit goal of recruiting new players in-person. Not a formal event — just a concentrated presence.

**The in-person pitch:**
> "You just played against [Ambassador Name] — you should rate him on Ball Up Top. Download it, I'll show you how. Takes 3 minutes."

Players who install and complete their first check-in during a court takeover have dramatically higher retention than players who download from a social media post. They experienced the product in context and completed the full loop with help.

**How to execute:**
1. Pick the court's peak day/time for Court Takeover Day 1.
2. 3–5 ambassadors show up, play normally.
3. After each game or during breaks, ambassadors show the app to new players and walk them through check-in.
4. Target: 10–20 new installs and first check-ins per takeover.

**Cost:** $0.

### Idea 4: Dorm / GroupMe / Discord Drops

**What it is:** Identify existing campus basketball communication channels — residence hall GroupMes, IM basketball team chats, campus rec Discord servers, Black Student Union chats, ROTC group chats — and post a short, personal, non-promotional message about Ball Up Top.

**Message template:**
> "Hey — I'm building an app for pickup basketball at [School] and just launched it at the rec center. It shows who's at the court right now and gives players a skill rating + archetype. Looking for people to try it. Would anyone be down to check in this week at [Court]?"

**Why it works:**
This audience is self-selected (basketball players in a basketball context) and the message feels human because it is human. Response rate and install conversion from warm group chats is 5–10x higher than from cold social media posts.

**How to find channels:**
- Ask ambassadors directly: "What GroupMes / Discords do you have for basketball?"
- r/[school] posts about intramural sports often link to Discord servers
- Campus rec department may have public Discord for recreational sports

**Cost:** $0. Time: 2–3 hours mapping channels + writing.

### Idea 5: Intramural Basketball Integration

**What it is:** Partner with campus intramural sports to introduce Ball Up Top to IM basketball participants — either as an official partner (check-in for IM games) or an unofficial presence (ambassador presence at IM games, post-game rating).

**The IM coordinator pitch:**
> "Ball Up Top gives intramural basketball players a skill rating and archetype based on their real games. It's free. We'd love to offer it to [School] IM basketball participants as a value-add. Players check in before their game, rate opponents after, and see their campus ranking."

**What IM basketball players get:**
- Team leaderboard screenshots (top-rated players on their IM team) for social sharing
- Access to the full campus rating system through IM game context
- A reason to keep using the app beyond IM season (pickup basketball sessions)

**Why this is high-value:**
IM basketball players are the most committed, organized segment of the campus basketball community. They've already demonstrated willingness to show up to organized games — the check-in and rating behaviors are more natural for them than for pure pickup players.

**Cost:** $0–$20 (optional printed flyers). Time: 2–4 hours outreach.

### Idea 6: Weatherproof QR Code Stickers at Courts

**What it is:** Small weatherproof stickers at the target court: "Who runs this court? Ball Up Top." with a QR code deep-linking to the court's page.

**Placement:**
- Backboard poles
- Bench areas
- Court entrance bulletin boards (tape, not adhesive, where required)
- Water fountain areas near courts

**Why it works:**
A player who is literally at a court, about to play, is the highest-intent Ball Up Top lead that exists. They have the problem (is anyone here? where do I rank?) at the exact moment they see the solution.

**Cost:** ~$30 for 100 weatherproof stickers. Time: 2 hours design + 1 hour placement.

### Idea 7: Campus Newspaper Feature

**What it is:** Pitch the campus newspaper sports section on a "local student builds pickup basketball app for [School] players" story. This runs as an editorial piece, not an ad.

**The pitch to the sports editor:**
> "Subject: Story idea — [School] student builds pickup basketball app for campus courts
>
> I'm a [School] student who built Ball Up Top — the first app that shows who's playing at the campus rec center right now and ranks every pickup basketball player on campus. We just launched here. The [School] leaderboard is live and the top player is currently [Name/rating]. Would this be a story your sports section would cover?"

**Expected outcome:** 1 story = 50–200 installs from a trusted, credible campus source. The "featured in [Campus Paper]" credibility also serves as social proof on future marketing materials.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 1–2 hours writing the pitch.

## Phase 2: University Expansion Ideas

### Idea 8: The Rival Campus Campaign

**What it is:** Once two campuses are active, launch a "who runs their campus better?" comparison campaign. Show the top-rated players at each school's court with a "who would win?" framing.

**Example:** "Purdue's #1 (84 overall) vs. IU's #1 (82 overall). Who's actually better?"

**Why it works:**
Campus rivalry is one of the highest-engagement social frames in college sports. Ball Up Top's leaderboard data is the raw material for a rivalry narrative that writes itself. No competition between the actual players needs to happen — the ratings tell the story.

**Distribution:** Ball Up Top Instagram + ambassador shares at both campuses. Tag both campus basketball communities. The rival campus sees the post and wants Ball Up Top to prove they're better.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 1 hour per rivalry post.

---

### Idea 9: Campus Newspaper Pipeline

**What it is:** Replicate the Idea 7 pitch at every new campus launch. Build a template and compress the outreach to 30 minutes per campus.

**The formula:** Same story, different school. "Local [School] student-built app that's already running at Purdue and Ohio State launches here."

By campus #3, Ball Up Top has credibility that makes the story easier to place: "The app that's already at Purdue and Ohio State is now at [School]."

---

### Idea 10: Campus-to-Campus Ambassador Recruitment

**What it is:** Ask top ambassadors at each campus: "Do you know any players at [Next Campus]?" Use warm introductions to identify the first ambassador lead at the next school.

**Why this compounds:** By campus #5, 60%+ of ambassador recruitment should come from existing network referrals. Each campus adds to the pool of warm connections to neighboring schools.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 15 minutes per outreach conversation.

---

### Idea 11: Greek Life and Athletic Dorm Targeting

**What it is:** At each new campus, identify 2–3 fraternities with known basketball cultures (often visible via IM basketball standings, social media, or word of mouth) and reach out to their social chairs or athletic officers.

**Why it works:**
Fraternities have pre-existing internal social networks and organized sports traditions. A single buy-in from an influential member cascades to the whole house. If one house adopts Ball Up Top, their 30–50 members who play ball are a ready-made cohort.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 2–3 hours per campus.

## Phase 3: National Growth Ideas

### Idea 12: "Ball Up Top Is Taking Over College Basketball" PR Narrative

**What it is:** Once 5+ campuses are active, pitch national tech and sports media on the broader story: "The app taking over pickup basketball at major college campuses."

**Target publications:**
- TechCrunch / The Verge (startup story: "Yik Yak meets Chess.com for college basketball")
- Sports Business Journal (sports tech angle)
- Student media aggregators (publications covering multiple campus stories)
- Local news at each active campus city

**Why the story works now:**
A single campus launch isn't national news. Five campuses — Purdue, Ohio State, Michigan, IU, Michigan State — is a pattern. "The Big Ten's pickup basketball scene has an app, and it's growing fast" is a story that didn't exist when you had one campus.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 5–10 hours per media outreach cycle.

---

### Idea 13: TikTok / Instagram UGC Campaign

**What it is:** Encourage players to post their archetype reveals on TikTok and Instagram with #BallUpTop. Repost the best ones from the Ball Up Top account. UGC is the acquisition engine.

**Format:** "Just got my basketball archetype on Ball Up Top and it's actually accurate" — player shows the reveal, shows their stats, tags a friend.

**Why it scales:**
Each player's TikTok reach extends to their campus social graph (classmates, teammates, other hoopers they know). A player with 800 followers posting their archetype reveal reaches ~50–200 people who share their context (college student, basketball player). That's the highest-quality lead possible.

**Cost:** $0. Time: 15 min/day monitoring tags and reposting.

---

### Idea 14: Season Championship Social Content

**What it is:** End-of-semester championship announcement posts for every active campus. "Season 1 Champion at [School]: [Name], [Archetype], [Overall] overall."

**Distribution:** Ball Up Top Instagram (all campuses), campus-specific Instagram accounts.

**Why it compounds:**
- The champion shares because they're being featured publicly
- Followers of the champion see Ball Up Top for the first time
- End-of-season urgency drives last-minute check-ins before the season closes
- Historical record builds: "Season 1 Champion at Purdue was [Name]" becomes campus basketball lore

**Cost:** $0. Time: 1 hour per championship post.

---

### Idea 15: Sneaker / Basketball Culture Brand Partnerships

**What it is:** Once 3+ campuses are active and engagement metrics are strong, approach basketball-adjacent brands for co-marketing. Ball Up Top's audience — verified, skill-identified, college basketball players — is a highly specific audience brands pay significant money to reach through traditional channels.

**First-tier targets (authentic fit):**
- AND1, Under Armour Basketball, Jordan Brand (DTC/social), SLAM magazine
- Basketball training platforms (Dribble2Much, Shot Mechanics YouTube channels)
- Court culture apparel brands (smaller brands with authentic street credibility)

**Second-tier targets (post scale):**
- Gatorade, Nike, major footwear brands — harder to activate at early stage but viable at 10+ campuses

**The pitch:** "Ball Up Top has [X] verified, skill-rated college basketball players at [N] campuses. These are not casual users — they've GPS-verified their presence at basketball courts, rated their opponents, and earned archetypes. We can offer [content partnership / branded seasonal award / archetype sponsored by X] to this exact audience."

**Cost:** $0 to pitch. Deal terms TBD.
