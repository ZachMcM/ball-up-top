# Viral & Engagement Campaign Strategy — Ball Up Top

*Last updated: 2026-04-11*
*Post-pivot. Campus-community model. Campaigns designed for campus basketball culture.*
*Core tension throughout: extrinsic rewards (cash) drive fast adoption; intrinsic rewards (status, identity) drive durable retention.*

---

## Strategic Framework

Ball Up Top campaigns operate on a three-tier reward structure:

```
Tier 1: Cash prizes → Launch week only. Cold-start tool. Not a product feature.
           ↓
Tier 2: Badge / recognition → Ongoing. Habit-building. Permanent status signals.
           ↓
Tier 3: Pure intrinsic → Long-term dominant. Archetype pride, leaderboard rank, rivalry, rep.
```

The goal of every campaign is to move players from Tier 1 motivation (cash) to Tier 3 motivation (intrinsic) before Tier 1 rewards are removed. If a player is still only checking in for a prize at Week 4, the campaign failed. If they're checking in because they care about their leaderboard rank, the campaign succeeded.

## The Founding Hooper Campaign (Launch Week — Permanent)

**What it is:** The first 50 players to complete their first check-in at a newly launched campus receive "Founding Hooper — [School] — Season 1" permanent profile badge.

**Why this is the right launch campaign:**
- **Genuine scarcity** — 50 is the real cap; after that it's gone forever
- **Identity-bearing** — "I was there when Ball Up Top started at [School]" is a statement of community membership and basketball credibility
- **No cash** — avoids the Overjustification Effect (extrinsic rewards undermining intrinsic motivation)
- **No gaming incentive** — completing a check-in doesn't benefit from gaming; it just requires showing up

**Execution:**
- Post a live counter to Instagram Stories throughout launch day: "38 of 50 Founding Hooper spots remaining"
- Ambassadors communicate Founding Hooper status in their personal pitches
- Badge displays permanently on profile — visible to any player who views the profile

## Campaign 1: Launch Week Leaderboard Prize (Cash — One Time Only)

**Name:** "[School] First Hoopers Week"

**Concept:** During the first 7 days of the campus launch, the top 3 players on the leaderboard by eligible metric win cash prizes.

**Prize structure:** $50 / $25 / $15 (Venmo/Cash App — pay within 48 hours of campaign end)

**Why $90 total is the right investment:**
If this campaign drives 80 new installs and 50 activations, the effective cost per activated user is $1.80. That is exceptional. This $90 is not charity — it's the most efficient marketing spend available.

**Eligibility gates (critical — prevent gaming):**
- Minimum 3 sessions completed during the campaign week
- Sessions must be at least 30 minutes each
- Must have ratings from at least 4 unique opponents
- Must have checked in at the target court at least once

**Winning metric:** Highest overall rating at campaign end among eligible players.

**Why eligibility gates matter — the Cobra Effect:**
Without gates, cash prizes attract the Cobra Effect: players recruit friends to check in together, rate each other at maximum, and split the prize. With gates (minimum sessions + unique opponents), the winner must have genuinely played real games against real opponents. Gaming requires showing up to the court and actually playing — which is exactly the behavior we want.

**Campaign leaderboard display:** Update every 6 hours. Show rank, name, overall rating, session count. Real-time visibility creates competition — the last 48 hours will generate disproportionate activity from players near the cutoff.

**Push notification sequence:**

| Trigger | Message |
|---------|---------|
| Player enters top 3 for first time | "You just broke into the top 3 at [Court] — #[N] with [X] days left." |
| Player drops a position | "You dropped to #[N+1]. [Player] just passed you." |
| 48 hours remaining | "48 hours left. You're [N] sessions from the top 3." |
| Final day | "Last day. Don't leave [Court] money on the table." |
| Campaign ends — winner | "#1 at [School] this week. $50 coming your way." |
| Campaign ends — missed | "You finished #[N]. Season 1 continues. Don't stop." |

**Post-campaign:** Announce winners publicly on Instagram with real name and archetype. This makes the reward real and visible — social proof that the prizes are genuine, which sets up future campaigns at new campuses.

**CRITICAL:** Do not repeat this cash prize campaign after launch week. It is a cold-start tool, not a product feature. Repeating it trains players to expect cash for using the product — the Overjustification Effect kicks in, and engagement collapses when prizes stop.

## Campaign 2: Check-In Streak Challenge (Ongoing — Intrinsic)

**Name:** "7-Day Streak"

**Concept:** Any player who checks in at least once per day for 7 consecutive days receives a permanent "Dedicated Hooper" badge on their profile.

**Why this works:**
- **Zeigarnik + Loss Aversion:** By Day 5, the fear of *losing* 5 days of progress is stronger than the badge reward itself. This is Duolingo's most powerful retention mechanic — streaks are psychologically sticky.
- **No gaming incentive:** A real-location GPS check-in at a real court cannot be gamed. You either show up or you lose the streak.
- **No cash:** Badge as reward preserves intrinsic motivation.

**Push notification sequence:**

| Day | Notification |
|-----|-------------|
| Day 3 | "3-day check-in streak at [Court]. Keep going." |
| Day 5 | "Day 5. Almost there. Two more days for the Dedicated Hooper badge." |
| Day 6 | "One more day. Don't break your streak now." |
| Day 7 (complete) | "7-day streak. Dedicated Hooper — that's yours now. Permanent." |
| Break detected | "Streak ended at [N] days. [Court] misses you. Start again today." |

## Campaign 3: First Loop Activation Sprint (Launch — Intrinsic)

**Name:** "Complete Your First Run"

**Concept:** Players who complete the full loop — check-in → check-out → submit at least 1 rating — within their first 7 days receive a "First Run" digital badge.

**Why this is the most strategically important campaign:**
The biggest funnel dropout in Ball Up Top is between download and first rating submission. A player who completes the full loop once has built the schema for doing it again. The activation rate is the North Star of the launch. This campaign directly attacks the most important conversion.

**UX implementation:**
- Show on home screen during first 7 days post-install as a "challenge" with progress indicator
- "Step 1: Check in ✓ | Step 2: Check out | Step 3: Rate at least 1 player"
- On completion: short animation (confetti or bar fill), badge reveal, immediate "Share your archetype card" prompt
- The share CTA immediately after badge completion is the highest-motivation moment for sharing

## Campaign 4: Referral Race — "Bring Your Crew" (Growth — Mixed)

**Name:** "Bring Your Crew"

**Concept:** Players earn rewards when friends they refer complete their first session (not just download — must complete a real check-in).

**Reward tiers:**
- 1 friend activated → "Crew Builder" badge
- 3 friends activated → $10 Venmo + "Connector" badge
- 5 friends activated → $25 Venmo + "Legend" profile border

**Why activation-gating is essential:**
Rewarding downloads incentivizes spam (recruit anyone with a phone). Rewarding activated users (first real session) requires that the referred player actually show up to a court and play. This is self-qualifying — only basketball players who want to use the product will complete a session.

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** Paying per download ($5 per code redemption) — this produces exactly the low-quality acquisition and rating gaming described above.

**Implementation:**
- Unique referral link generated in profile
- Real-time "Your crew" section showing referred friends and their status
- Reward delivered within 24 hours of friend's first session completion

## Campaign 5: Court Championship Season (Ongoing — Intrinsic)

**Name:** "Season [N] Championship"

**Concept:** Every 3 months (aligned to academic semesters), the highest-rated player at each campus court at the end of the season is crowned Season Champion. They receive a permanent season trophy on their profile and a featured Instagram post.

**Season calendar:**
- Fall Season: September 1 – December 1
- Spring Season: January 15 – April 30
- Summer Season (optional): May 15 – August 15

**Why seasons are the long-term retention engine:**
- Creates a narrative arc (there's always a season in progress with an end date that matters)
- Solves the post-launch engagement cliff (launch week is the spike; what keeps players in week 6? the season)
- Season defense behavior: players near the top play more to protect their standing
- Re-engagement hook at each new season start: "New season, new race — the leaderboard resets"
- Annual ritual: "Season 2 champion at [School] was [Name]" becomes campus basketball lore that compounds

**Season reset mechanics:**
- Leaderboard positions reset each season — new race begins
- Individual ratings carry forward — your skill reputation is permanent
- Founding Hooper badge never resets — it marks the origin

**End-of-season activation:**
- Announcement post on Instagram featuring the champion (with permission)
- In-app notification to all players: "[Name] won Season [N] at [Court]. Season [N+1] starts [date] — the leaderboard is reset. New race."
- Season trophy visible permanently on champion's profile

## Campaign 6: Archetype Virality Amplifier (Ongoing — Intrinsic)

**Name:** "Share Your Archetype"

**Concept:** Players who share their archetype card to Instagram Stories and tag @balluptop (or @balluptop_[school]) receive a small in-app feature: full rating history breakdown unlocked (usually gated at fewer data points).

**Why this works:**
- Sharing an archetype card is something players *want* to do — the reward prompts them to do it *now*
- Each share is a direct acquisition event: a basketball player's Instagram Story reaches ~100–500 followers, a meaningful percentage of whom play ball
- The feature unlock feels like receiving something earned, not a cash transaction — preserves authenticity

**Anti-pattern:** Do NOT pay cash for archetype shares. The social share is already a favor to the product — reciprocating with recognition is appropriate; cash turns it transactional and undermines the authenticity.

## Campaign 7: Court Rival System (Product-Embedded — Intrinsic)

**Name:** "The Rivalry"

**Concept:** When a player has played against the same opponent 3+ times, they receive a notification: "You and [Name] have gone head to head [N] times. Who's winning the rivalry?" with a comparison card showing their ratings across those sessions.

**Why this is the most retention-efficient campaign:**
- No marginal cost — it uses data Ball Up Top already has
- Creates a persistent, court-specific reason to return ("I need to play [Name] again")
- Generates shareable content: "Me vs. [Name] — [N] sessions at [Court]. Here's the story."
- Drives return to specific courts (rivalry is court-specific)

**UX implementation:**
- Automatic trigger: after 3 sessions at the same court with the same opponent
- Notification: "You and [Name] are becoming rivals at [Court]"
- Profile section: "Rivals" showing top 3 recurring opponents with mutual rating history
- Shareable rivalry card: "[Archetype A] vs. [Archetype B] — [N] sessions at [Court]"

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Ongoing Cash Prize Campaigns
**What it looks like:** "Every week, top 3 players win money."
**Why it fails:** Trains players to expect cash. When it ends (and it must), engagement collapses. Creates permanent gaming incentive.

### Rating-Count Based Prizes
**What it looks like:** "Most ratings submitted wins."
**Why it fails:** Directly incentivizes session farming and collusion. Corrupts the rating system's data quality permanently.

### Follower/Download Referral Rewards
**What it looks like:** "$5 for every person who downloads with your code."
**Why it fails:** Incentivizes spam referrals to non-basketball players. Inflates download numbers without activations.

### Too Many Simultaneous Campaigns
**What it looks like:** Running 4 campaigns simultaneously during launch week.
**Why it fails:** Players don't know which behavior to optimize for. Urgency dilutes. Pick one primary campaign per time window.

## Recommended Campaign Stack by Phase

### Launch Week (Days 1–7)
- **Primary:** Founding Hooper status (scarcity framing, permanent badge)
- **Primary (if budget exists):** Leaderboard Prize — "First Hoopers Week" ($90)
- **Background:** First Loop Activation Sprint (badge for completing first session)

### Weeks 2–4 (Post-Cash Transition)
- **Primary:** Check-In Streak Challenge (7-day streak → badge)
- **Secondary:** Referral Race — "Bring Your Crew" (activation-gated)
- **Background:** Archetype Share Amplifier

### Month 2+ (Community Building)
- **Primary:** Court Rival System (automatic, product-embedded)
- **Background:** Season Championship countdown (building anticipation)
- **Content:** Season standings posts, player spotlights

### End of Semester (Season Championship)
- **Primary:** Season Championship — featured Instagram announcement, trophy badge
- **Secondary:** New season launch — "leaderboard resets, new race begins"
- **Content:** Champion spotlight, next season framing
