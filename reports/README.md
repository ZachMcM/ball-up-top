## Key Conclusions

**Feasibility verdict:** Ball Up Top is technically sound and addresses real, unmet needs. The primary feasibility risk is the cold-start problem — the app needs players at the same court simultaneously to deliver real-time value, and achieving that requires manual seeding before organic growth can take over.

**The one thing that matters:** Getting 5–10 players checked in at 2 Purdue courts reliably on an active day. Everything else is a proxy for this.

**The launch sequence:**
1. Recruit 10–15 ambassador players at 2 Purdue courts (CoRec + 1 outdoor)
2. 2-week manual pre-seeding before any public promotion
3. Launch publicly with leaderboards already populated
4. "Founding Player" scarcity drives first 50 installs
5. Archetype share cards drive organic virality
6. Validate KPIs for 30 days before expanding to IU/Notre Dame
