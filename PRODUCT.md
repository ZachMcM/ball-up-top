# Product

## Register

product

## Users

College basketball players at campus recreation courts. They show up to play pickup, want to know who else is there, and care about their reputation among regulars. Context: standing at the court, phone in hand, deciding whether to stay or leave based on who's playing.

## Product Purpose

GPS-verified check-in for pickup basketball. Players build skill profiles through peer ratings (45-99 scale, 40+ archetypes). One court per campus, leaderboard-driven. The app answers: "Who's at the court right now?" and "How good are they?"

## Brand Personality

Competitive, Social, Sharp. The energy of trash talk between friends. Confident without being aggressive. Stats-forward like 2K, but grounded in real reputation earned on the court.

## Anti-references

- Gaming leaderboard UIs: no neon colors, no heavy gradients, no glowing borders, no "rank up" animations
- Overly designed fitness apps: no lifestyle imagery, no motivational quotes, no progress rings everywhere
- Generic social apps: no infinite feeds, no algorithmic content, no engagement dark patterns

## Design Principles

1. **Court-first**: Every screen should feel like you're one tap from playing. No buried actions.
2. **Earned reputation**: Stats and ratings are sacred. Display them with weight, not decoration.
3. **Real-time presence**: Live states (who's playing now) are the core value. Make them unmissable.
4. **Social proof over social features**: Show who's good, not who's popular. No likes, no comments, no followers.
5. **Utility over engagement**: Help players find games, not spend time in the app.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target. Standard mobile accessibility: touch targets 44pt minimum, sufficient color contrast, support for system font scaling.
